The competition
WHAT IS
THE IPO?

The International Philosophy Olympiad is an annual global competition where students write original philosophical essays and defend their ideas before an international jury. Over 60 countries compete. It is one of the most intellectually demanding competitions a high school student can enter.

Students receive four philosophical quotations on the day of the competition and choose one as the starting point for an original essay — written without reference materials, judged on argumentative rigour, precision, and genuine philosophical thinking.

Top essays advance to an oral defence before the international jury. Gold, silver, and bronze medals are awarded. IPO recognition carries real weight with university admissions teams worldwide, particularly for PPE, philosophy, law, and humanities programmes.

"The IPO rewards students who can think independently, argue precisely, and engage seriously with ideas — not those who have simply memorised more philosophy."
